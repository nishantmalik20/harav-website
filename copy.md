**Considered, warm, and quietly opulent — an editorial hush, like the menu of a candle-lit supper room. Glamour through restraint: short sentences, gold-lit imagery, and time that belongs entirely to the guest.**

**Non-negotiable voice rules (from the design system):**
- **Sentence case** for headlines and buttons. ALL CAPS only for eyebrows, labels, the wordmark.
- **Em dashes** for cadence. **Full stops, never exclamation marks.** Fragments are welcome.
- **"You"** for the guest, always. **"We"** for the salon, sparingly. Never "I."
- Never "ladies / girls / babes / hun." **No emoji, anywhere — ever.**
- Sensory and material: gold, warm light, linen, caramel, cedar. Numerals as numerals (`60 min`, `$49`).
- New business → **no fabricated proof.** Anywhere a real number/review is needed, it's marked `[client to confirm]`.

## Reviews & Testimonials — handling for a brand-new business
Harav has **no real reviews yet** and **no Google Business Profile**. We will **not publish fabricated reviews as if they were real** — under Canada's Competition Act (and the U.S. FTC's 2024 rule) fake testimonials are deceptive marketing, and for a small local salon they're easy to spot and costly to trust. The site still needs to feel proven, so we do it honestly:

- **Until real reviews exist:** lean on the true signals already written — Khushi's 5+ years, the women-only room, the careful/hygienic craft, newly opened in Fort Garry. **Hide** the design's star-rating pill and review counts rather than faking them.
- **The samples below are layout placeholders only** — clearly marked, to be **replaced with real, consented guest reviews before launch.**
- **Fast, honest way to get real proof (recommended):** create the free Google Business Profile now; invite first-weeks guests to leave an honest review (a small thank-you for their time is fine — never pay for *positive* reviews); surface them as they arrive. A handful of genuine local reviews will outperform any invented ones.
